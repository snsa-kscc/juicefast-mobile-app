Iron-Boosting Spinach & Lentil Stew
Category: Postpartum Nutrition  |  Tags: Iron-rich, One-pot, Freezer-friendly, Dairy-free
  

Why It's Great for Postpartum Nutrition
Replenishes iron lost during birth. Lentils provide plant-based protein for milk production. Easy to eat one-handed while nursing.
Quick Info
Prep: 10 min  |  Cook: 25 min  |  Servings: 4  |  Difficulty: Easy
Nutrition (per serving)
Calories: 320 kcal  |  Protein: 18g  |  Carbs: 42g  |  Fat: 8g  |  Fiber: 12g
Bonus: Iron: 6mg (35% DV)
Ingredients
• 1 cup red lentils, rinsed
• 200g fresh spinach
• 1 can (400g) diced tomatoes
• 1 medium onion, diced
• 3 garlic cloves, minced
• 2 tbsp olive oil
• 1 tsp cumin
• ½ tsp turmeric
• 4 cups vegetable broth
• Salt & pepper to taste
• Squeeze of lemon juice
Instructions
1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Sauté onion until soft (3-4 min).
2. Add garlic, cumin, and turmeric. Stir for 30 seconds until fragrant.
3. Pour in lentils, tomatoes, and broth. Bring to boil, then reduce heat.
4. Simmer 20 minutes until lentils are tender.
5. Stir in spinach until wilted. Season with salt, pepper, and lemon.
6. Serve warm. Store leftovers up to 4 days or freeze for 3 months.
Tips & Variations
• Add a fried egg on top for extra protein
• Batch cook and freeze in portions for exhausted days
• Pairs well with crusty bread or rice

Salmon & Sweet Potato Power Bowl  
Category: Postpartum Nutrition  |  Tags: Omega-3 rich, Brain health, High protein, Gluten-free
Why It's Great for Postpartum Nutrition
Omega-3 fatty acids support brain development in breastfed babies. Sweet potatoes provide sustained energy and vitamin A for recovery.
Quick Info
Prep: 15 min  |  Cook: 30 min  |  Servings: 2  |  Difficulty: Easy
Nutrition (per serving)
Calories: 480 kcal  |  Protein: 32g  |  Carbs: 38g  |  Fat: 22g  |  Fiber: 7g
Bonus: Omega-3: 2.5g, Vitamin A: 380% DV
Ingredients
• 2 salmon fillets (150g each)
• 2 medium sweet potatoes, cubed
• 2 cups arugula
• 1 avocado, sliced
• 2 tbsp olive oil
• 2 tbsp tahini
• 1 lemon, juiced
• Salt, pepper, paprika
Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 200°C. Toss sweet potato cubes with 1 tbsp olive oil, salt, and paprika.
2. Roast sweet potatoes for 20 minutes until tender.
3. Season salmon with salt and pepper. Pan-sear in remaining olive oil, 4 min per side.
4. Arrange arugula in bowls. Top with sweet potato, flaked salmon, and avocado.
5. Drizzle with tahini mixed with lemon juice. Serve immediately.
Tips & Variations
• Use canned salmon for a quicker version
• Prep sweet potatoes ahead and refrigerate
• Add quinoa for extra sustenance

Lactation Cookie Energy Bites
Category: Postpartum Nutrition  |  Tags:  , No-bake, Grab-and-go, Oat-based
  

Why It's Great for Postpartum Nutrition
Oats and brewer's yeast are galactagogues that support milk production. Perfect one-handed snack for nursing sessions.
Quick Info
Prep: 15 min  |  Cook: 0 min (chill 30 min)  |  Servings: 20 bites  |  Difficulty: Easy
Nutrition (per serving)
Calories: 95 kcal  |  Protein: 3g  |  Carbs: 12g  |  Fat: 5g  |  Fiber: 2g
Bonus: B vitamins from brewer's yeast
Ingredients
• 1½ cups rolled oats
• ½ cup peanut butter
• ⅓ cup honey
• 2 tbsp brewer's yeast
• 2 tbsp ground flaxseed
• ¼ cup mini chocolate chips
• 1 tsp vanilla extract
• Pinch of salt
Instructions
1. Combine all ingredients in a large bowl. Mix until fully combined.
2. If mixture is too dry, add 1 tbsp milk. If too wet, add more oats.
3. Roll into 20 small balls using your hands.
4. Place on a lined baking sheet. Refrigerate for 30 minutes.
5. Store in airtight container in fridge for up to 1 week.
Tips & Variations
• Substitute almond butter for peanut-free version
• Add dried cranberries or coconut flakes
• Keep a container by your nursing station

Postpartum Recovery Chicken Congee
Category: Postpartum Nutrition  |  Tags: Traditional healing, Easy to digest, Warming, Protein-rich
  

Why It's Great for Postpartum Nutrition
Traditional postpartum food across Asian cultures. Gentle on digestion while providing protein and hydration. Ginger warms the body and aids recovery.
Quick Info
Prep: 10 min  |  Cook: 45 min  |  Servings: 4  |  Difficulty: Easy
Nutrition (per serving)
Calories: 290 kcal  |  Protein: 22g  |  Carbs: 35g  |  Fat: 6g  |  Fiber: 1g
Bonus: Easily digestible protein
Ingredients
• 1 cup jasmine rice
• 8 cups chicken broth
• 2 chicken thighs, bone-in
• 2 inches fresh ginger, sliced
• 3 green onions, chopped
• 1 tbsp sesame oil
• Soy sauce to taste
• White pepper
Instructions
1. Rinse rice until water runs clear.
2. Bring broth to boil with chicken thighs and ginger slices.
3. Add rice, reduce heat to low. Simmer 45 minutes, stirring occasionally.
4. Remove chicken, shred meat, and return to pot.
5. Ladle into bowls. Top with green onions, sesame oil, soy sauce, and white pepper.
Tips & Variations

• Freeze in portions for easy reheating
• Add a soft-boiled egg for extra nutrition

IMAGE PROMPT
Overhead shot of a deep bowl with golden miso broth, cubed tofu, shredded chicken, wakame seaweed, and sliced mushrooms. Steam visible, light wooden table, Japanese-style minimal presentation, small dish of miso paste as prop.
Why It's Great for Postpartum Nutrition
Miso provides probiotics for gut health and immune support. Hydrating and warming. Easy to digest during recovery.
Quick Info
Prep: 10 min  |  Cook: 15 min  |  Servings: 2  |  Difficulty: Easy
Nutrition (per serving)
Calories: 240 kcal  |  Protein: 24g  |  Carbs: 14g  |  Fat: 10g  |  Fiber: 3g
Bonus: Probiotics from miso
Ingredients
• 4 cups water
• 3 tbsp white miso paste
• 150g cooked chicken, shredded
• 100g firm tofu, cubed
• 2 tbsp dried wakame seaweed
• 1 cup mushrooms, sliced
• 2 green onions, chopped
Instructions
1. Bring water to a simmer (not boiling) in a pot.
2. Add mushrooms and wakame. Cook for 3 minutes.
3. Add tofu and chicken. Heat through for 2 minutes.
4. Remove from heat. Whisk in miso paste until dissolved.
5. Ladle into bowls. Garnish with green onions. Serve warm.
Tips & Variations
• Never boil miso - it kills the probiotics
• Add noodles for a more filling meal
• Use any leftover protein you have on hand

Watermelon Mint Agua Fresca
Category: Mocktails  |  Tags: Mexican, Super refreshing, Summer, Low calorie
  





IMAGE PROMPT
Overhead shot of a pink watermelon drink in a tall glass, fresh mint leaves floating, small watermelon wedge on rim. Bright summer light, watermelon slice and mint bunch as props, Mexican fiesta-inspired colorful styling.
Why It's Great for Mocktails
Watermelon is 92% water - extremely hydrating. Traditional Mexican refreshment. Very low calorie.
Quick Info
Prep: 10 min  |  Cook: 0 min  |  Servings: 4  |  Difficulty: Easy
Nutrition (per serving)
Calories: 50 kcal  |  Protein: 1g  |  Carbs: 12g  |  Fat: 0g  |  Fiber: 1g
Bonus: Extremely hydrating
Ingredients
• 4 cups watermelon, cubed and seeded
• 2 cups cold water
• 2 tbsp lime juice
• 1 tbsp honey (optional)
• Fresh mint leaves
• Ice
• Watermelon wedges for garnish
Instructions
1. Blend watermelon until smooth.
2. Strain through fine mesh sieve, pressing to extract all liquid.
3. Stir in water, lime juice, and honey if using.
4. Add torn mint leaves.
5. Refrigerate until cold.
6. Serve over ice with watermelon wedge and mint.
Tips & Variations
• No need to strain for more rustic version
• Freeze watermelon cubes as ice
• Add jalapeño slice for spicy Mexican twist
